Balazs Zelity

I am a PhD candidate in economics at Brown University. My research focuses on policy-relevant issues in the areas of economic growth and macroeconomics. My work has explored how demographics, institutions, and environmental policies shape the macroeconomic trajectories of countries in the medium-to-long run.

My approach to research combines empirical and theoretical tools. I work extensively with causal inference techniques as well as with general equilibrium models.

I am on the job market in 2019-2020 and will be available for interviews at the ASSA Meeting in San Diego, and at the European Job Market Meeting in Rotterdam.
