Balazs Zelity

I am a PhD candidate in economics at Brown University. My research focuses on policy-relevant issues in the areas of economic growth and macroeconomics. My work has explored how demographics, political systems and institutions, and environmental policies shape the macroeconomic trajectories of countries in the medium-to-long run.

My approach to research combines empirical and theoretical tools. I work extensively with causal inference techniques as well as with general equilibrium models, which I analyse mathematically and computationally.
