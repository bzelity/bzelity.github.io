Teaching experience as Primary Instructor

Investments II (Financial Derivatives)

Brown University - Spring 2019, Fall 2018
[course description]
This is an advanced undergraduate class introducing students to financial derivatives. We focus on forwards, futures, swaps, and options. We discuss how these securities are priced, where and how they are traded, and how they can be used to hedge, speculate, and take advantage of arbitrage opportunities. The class concludes with an extensive discussion of the Black-Scholes-Merton model, and the hedging of options portfolios using the Greeks.

The Wealth of Nations: Why Are Some Countries Richer Than Others?

Brown University - Summer 2019
[course description]
This is a summer pre-college class covering the most fundamental ideas in the field of economic growth. We begin by exploring the role of physical and human capital accumulation in explaining disparities in income per capita across the globe and over time. We then discuss the role of productivity, and the difference between input-driven and productivity-driven economic growth. To round off the class, we examine the fundamental determinants of growth such as geography, institutions, and culture.
